Giles Murray was born in London, England in 1966. Trained originally as a classicist, he has been living in Tokyo since 1991. He has produced a number of original books, translations and online education projects which are either profiled on, or accessible from, this website.

Giles's first literary venture was an ambitious project called ACID KANJ. Consisting of a colour booklet with a stereo CD, ACID KANJI was designed to teach Japanese characters and vocabulary by linking kanji ideograms with musical archetypes. You can see the logos and the artwork (Kanji martyrdom, Kanji lunatic asylum etc.) on the right.

Instant Business Japanese was published in March 1997. It was praised by the Financial Times as "a down-to-earth insight into Japanese business" and by the Asahi Evening News for "decoding many of the idiomatic words and expressions common in the Japanese business world, but which are not taught at language schools." Centered around the story of a word-processor maker reinventing itself as a maker of cool multimedia PCs, Instant Business Japanese teaches you everything need to know to deal with your Japanese business counterparts on an equal footing.

In May 1998 Giles produced an interactive children's guidebook for the London Science Museum exhibition in Japan. Co-financed with the Yomiuri Shimbun, the guidebook was written by Julia Murray, designed by Naoko Ito (creator of this website), and illustrated by Peter Prout and Tadashi Nomura, who worked on 13 Secrets for Speaking Fluent Japanese. The user-friendly guidebook sold over 14,000 copies. The cover, featuring Newton, Bell, Concorde and Arkwright's spinning-machine, is reproduced on the right.

13 Secrets for Speaking Fluent Japanese was published in March 1999. It was described as "nifty, unconventional... entertaining" by the Daily Yomiuri newspaper, and is currently in its sixth printing.

May 1999 saw the publication of Giles' bilingual guide to the UK , All you wanted to know about the UK. Giles also appeared as a presenter in the video version of Japanese for Busy People and, together with AJALT (Association for Japanese Language Teaching), is co-author of the Video Guide.

In 2000 Giles and the 13 Secrets Team produced William Blake Interactive, an extensive website on William Blake for London's Tate Gallery, complete with audio recordings, a pop-up game and original photographs. The site was featured on BBC radio and selected as a USA Today "Hot Site."

On a more frivolous note, the next year saw the first volumes of Giles' translation of Ken Akamatsu's best-selling Love Hina comic hit the book stores. Eventually a total of eight bilingual comics about the adventures of the nerdy pervert Keitaro were published.

In 2002 Giles' translation of Master Modeler, the autobiography of Shunsaku Tamiya, eponymous founder of the great model company, and Japan by Numbers, a statistical overview of Japanese society, were both published.

In January 2003, Breaking into Japanese Literature came out. Featuring seven celebrated tales by Japanese masters of the short story, it provides students with all the back-up they need to start reading modern Japanese classics. It includes specially commisioned illustrations and MP3 sound files for free Internet download.
